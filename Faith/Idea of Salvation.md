## B. We cannot save ourselves

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - Matthew 7:21-23
>     
>     ### **Concerning Self-Deception**
>     
>     **21** “Not every one who says to me, ‘Lord, Lord,’ shall enter the kingdom of heaven, but he who does the will of my Father who is in heaven. **22** On that day many will say to me, ‘Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in your name, and cast out demons in your name, and do many mighty works in your name?’ **23** And then will I declare to them, ‘I never knew you; depart from me, you evildoers.’

: Good works is not unimportant, it is actually the result of you having faith in following God’s will. “Faith results to good works”. If you do not have such faith to follow the will of the Father, good works is insufficient.

</aside>

# 2. A free gift waiting to be received and fulfilled daily: Salvation by Jesus is offered freely to us

## A. Salvation is treated as a Gift by God (no payment needed)

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - Ephesians 2:8-9
>     
>     **8** For by grace you have been saved through faith; and this is not your own doing, it is the gift of God— **9** not because of works, lest any man should boast.
>     

Context:

: Paul is writing to the church of Ephesus, consisting of Jews and Gentiles, to discuss about how salvation works.

: Ephesians 2:1-7 generally talks about how humanity is damned by its sins. Consequently, Paul mentions in verses 8-9 that humanity is saved by Jesus, that is unmerited (as a gift).

: On the next verse 10, Paul clarifies that while we are not saved by good works, we are saved to do good works.

> Doctrinal Basis
> 
> - CCC 1996
>     
>     "Our justification comes from the grace of God. Grace is favor, the **free and undeserved help** that God gives us"
>     

<aside> 💡

: Salvation is given to those who believe in Jesus AND in consequence, live their lives following His teachings (good works)

: Good works is not the cause, it is the proof and result of salvation

</aside>

## B. Salvatis there, you jon iust have to accept it through Baptism

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - John 3:5
>     
>     **5** Jesus answered, “Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God.
>     

Context:

: Jesus is teaching Nicodemus (a Pharisee) that physical birth is insufficient to go to heaven. People needs a spiritual rebirth through water and the Spirit (baptism) to belong to God’s Kingdom.

> Doctrinal Basis
> 
> - (**CCC 977**)
>     
>     "Baptism is the first and chief sacrament of forgiveness of sins”
>     

<aside> 💡

: Salvation is accessible and can be received by both Jews and Gentiles

: We only need to accept it through baptism, one of the holy sacraments, that Jesus commanded us to do (a good work)

</aside>

## C. Receiving Salvation is not a entrance ticket to Heaven, it is a lifelong commitment

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - Galatians 5:6
>     
>     **6** For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor uncircumcision is of any avail, but faith working through love.
>     

Context:

: Paul says that Jesus wants us to have faith that expresses itself in how you treat others (love is a good work), instead of external rituals like circumcision.

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - James 2:17
>     
>     **17** So faith by itself, if it has no works, is dead.
>     

Context:

: James rebukes those who claim to have faith but does not do good works (i.e. ignoring the poor).

: Faith is not real if good works do not follow.

> Doctrinal Basis
> 
> - CCC 1815
>     
>     "When it is deprived of hope and love, faith does not fully unite the believer to Christ”
>     

<aside> 💡

: You cannot separate faith from good works

: Good works are results and proofs of legitimate faith to God. Without such, faith is invalid, thus it does not save.

</aside>

# 3. Misinterpretations

## A. Sola Fide (Faith Alone)

### Counterargument:

"We are saved by faith alone (_Sola Fide_); adding works as a requirement suggests Christ’s sacrifice wasn't enough."

### Rebuttal:

### 1. Reclarification that Good works does not result to Salvation, but Good works cannot be separated from Real Faith to God

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - James 2:24
>     
>     **24** You see that a man is justified by works and not by faith alone.
>     

Context:

: James is writing for the believers who thought that having right belief and faith to God is enough, and that behavior did not matter.

: He mentioned that faith is not living (dead), and cannot save people if good works as its result is absent.

> Doctrinal Basis
> 
> - CCC 1815
>     
>     "Faith apart from works is dead... it does not fully unite the believer to Christ and does not make him a living member of his Body”
>     

<aside> 💡

: Good works by man will never be able to save people from damnation.

: Faith cannot save if good works, as a proof of real faith, is absent.

</aside>

### 2. Salvation is NOT a one-time deal, (Once Saved Always Saved is wrong), but it is a commitment

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - Matthew 24:13
>     
>     ### **Shining as Lights in the World**
>     
>     **12** Therefore, my beloved, as you have always obeyed, so now, not only as in my presence but much more in my absence, work out your own salvation with fear and trembling;
>     

Context:

: Jesus warned His disciples that trials and false prophets will eventually come, and emphasized that salvation requires enduring lifelong commitment.

: He mentioned that faith is not living (dead), and cannot save people if good works as its result is absent.

> Scriptural Basis
> 
> - Philippians 2:12
>     
>     **13** But he who endures to the end will be saved.
>     

Context:

: Paul told the Philippians to live out your salvation everyday.

: It does not mean to earn your salvation, but to not take it for granted.

> Doctrinal Basis
> 
> - CCC 2002
>     
>     “God’s free initiative demands man’s free response.”
>     
> - Council of Trent, Session 6, Canon 9
>     
>     “If anyone says that the sinner is justified by faith alone… let him be anathema.”
>     
> - _Lumen Gentium_ 14
>     
>     “They could not be saved who, knowing that the Catholic Church was founded by God through Christ, would refuse to enter or remain in it.”
>     
> - Joint Declaration on the Doctrine of Justification, §15 (1999 with Lutheran Protestant Church)
>     
>     “By grace alone, in faith in Christ’s saving work and not because of any merit on our part, we are accepted by God… Such faith is active in love.”
>     

<aside> 💡

: Salvation is a free gift that needs to be responded everyday with consequential good works, that is, faith is always accompanied by obedience with Christ’s teachings.

</aside>
