# 1. The Principle of Divine Authority

## 1.1. Christ established a visible and authoritative church

> Scriptural Basis

> - Matthew 16:17-19
> 
>     17 Jesus said to him in reply, “Blessed are you, Simon son of Jonah. For flesh and blood has not revealed this to you, but my heavenly Father. 
>     
>     18 And so I say to you, you are Peter, and upon this rock I will build my church, and the gates of the netherworld shall not prevail against it. 
>     
>     19 I will give you the keys to the kingdom of heaven.Whatever you bind on earth shall be bound in heaven; and whatever you loose on earth shall be loosed in heaven.”

Context
: When Jesus with His disciples went to Philippi, a region of several rock cliffs dedicated to pagan gods, He renamed Simon to Peter and promises to build His church upon him.

Old Testament Linearity
: In Isaiah 22:22 
>	22 I will place the key of the House of David on his shoulder;  
>	what he opens, no one will shut,  
>	what he shuts, no one will open.
: A Prime Minister (consider Peter) is given the keys of the kingdom by the Davidic King (consider Jesus' lineage with David), such that "binding and loosing" is the rabinnic authority of forbiding or permiting actions and teachings (consider the role of the Pope).


## 1.2. Apostolic Authority and the Magisterium

Scriptural Basis

> - Luke 10:16
> 	16 Whoever listens to you listens to me. Whoever rejects you rejects me. And whoever rejects me rejects the one who sent me.”

Context
: Jesus is commissioning the 72 disciples, deploying them to preach in nearby towns.

Historical Context
: The apostles can be compared to the Near Eastern concept of emissaries ('shaliah'), which possesses legal authority of the one who sent them.
: To disrespect a messenger of a lord or king is a direct declaration of war against the one who sent them.

Magisterium
: The official teaching authority of the Catholic church.
: This is passed down from the apostles to its disciples and to the bishops that are in communion with them.
: Listening to the Church's authentic teachers is also to listen to Jesus Himself tangibly (consider the concept of emissaries and messengers in Near East tradition)

# 1.3. The Church as the Pillar and Foundation of Truth

Scriptural Basis

> - 1 Timothy 3:15
> 	15 But if I should be delayed, you should know how to behave in the household of God, which is the church of the living God, the pillar and foundation of truth.

Context
: Paul is writing a pastoral letter to a young bishop Timothy, as Timothy is being tasked to govern the chaotic church of Ephesus.

Historical Context
: Ephesus has the massive Temple of Artemis with its famous 127 pillars that hold the roof. Paul used an imagery of the same pillar to describe the Church.

Keypoint
: The Scripture points outside of itself to the Church as the final interpreter and preserver of the faith.

# 2. Misinterpretations

## 2.1. Sola Scriptura (Scripture Alone)

: Sola Scriptura refers to the Scripture being the only divine authority.

### 2.1.1. Rebuttal 1: Scripture itself commands us to follow sacred tradition

> - 2 Thessalonians 2:15
> 	15 Therefore, brothers, stand firm and hold fast to the traditions that you were taught, either by an oral statement or by a letter of ours.

Context
: St. Paul is writing to the Christians in Thessalonica as there are rumors spreading that the day of the Lord has already come.
: As a response, St. Paul reminded the thessalonians to not consider rumors, but instead follow the apostolic teaching in the form of oral and written teachings.


### The Word of God Existed Long Before the Bible

> - 2 Timothy 2:2
> 	2 And what you heard from me through many witnesses entrust to faithful people who will have the ability to teach others as well.

Context
: This is one of St. Paul's final letter before his execution. It is about how Christianity will be continued after the death of the apostles.
: St. Paul did not tell Timothy to make a Bible, but he said that the Church will continue from Paul to Timothy, and then to the 'faithful men', and then to the 'others as well', implying apostolic succession.

Keypoint
: There is no Bible for the first centuries of the Church. Christians were guided and fortified by oral tradition preserved by the apostles to its disciples through generations.
